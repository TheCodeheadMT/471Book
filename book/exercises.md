# Exercises

In the Air Force, we train how we fight. Understanding the theory behind machine learning algorithms is only the first step; the true test is applying those concepts to dynamic, real-world scenarios. In a multi-domain operational environment, AI is not a "black box" that makes decisions for you—it is a tool that requires human understanding, oversight, and trust. 

When an algorithm flags a radar contact as a threat or categorizes an intercepted transmission as urgent, you must understand the exact mathematical mechanics driving that output. A miscalculation in the bias-variance tradeoff or a failure to account for the independence assumption isn't just a coding error; it can mean grounded aircraft, wasted munitions, or compromised intelligence. 

The following exercises are designed to test your tactical comprehension of the algorithms covered in this module. Before relying on Python libraries like `scikit-learn` to do the heavy lifting, try to calculate these foundational metrics by hand. By working through the math step-by-step, you will build the intuition necessary to deploy, debug, and trust these systems in the battlespace. 

Grab a calculator, review your formulas, and proceed to the problem sets.
